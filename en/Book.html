    Le Guide de l'eau": A cry of alarm for the preservation of our vital resource

    In her book, Céline Rouyrre reveals the crucial issues surrounding water and proposes practical solutions for its preservation.

    Book Water Guide

    In her book ‘Le Guide de l'eau’, Céline Rouyrre sounds the alarm about one of the major challenges of the 21st century: preserving our water resources. This book, published by Editions du Seuil, is an essential handbook for understanding the vital importance of water and the dangers that threaten it. The author highlights some alarming facts about water wastage. She points out, for example, that a simple dripping tap can lead to a loss of almost 100 litres a day.  

    "Unless we all take action, there can be no salvation.    Figures that make you wonder" 

    These figures, at first sight insignificant, take on a whole new dimension when multiplied on the scale of a city or a country. Rouyrre stresses the vital nature of water, pointing out that only 3% of the Earth's water is fresh, the rest being salty. This relative scarcity of drinking water makes the fight against waste and pollution all the more crucial.  

    Practical solutions for a sustainable future

    The book is not content to simply make pessimistic observations. It proposes practical solutions for preserving this precious resource. The author stresses the importance of responsible action on a daily basis, asserting that there is ‘no salvation’ without everyone taking action. ‘Le Guide de l'eau’ also looks at the threats to drinking water quality, such as pesticides and nitrates. These pollutants, which affect not only our health but also the environment, are presented as major challenges to be met.

    Short biography:

    Céline Rouyrre is a French author renowned for her commitment to environmental issues, particularly those relating to water management. She has devoted part of her career to raising public awareness of the importance of preserving water resources and the challenges associated with managing them. .
